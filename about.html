About Me

Born in the Himalayas

I grew up amid the Himalayan forests, trained as a forester and remote‑sensing ecologist, and now apply AI‑driven geospatial tools to monitor and protect our planet.

Science & Spirituality

My work bridges the analytical precision of science with the contemplative depth of spirituality. I believe true sustainability must be rooted not only in data, but also in ethics and reverence for all life.

Spiritual Journey

Over time, I’ve drawn strength and insight from multiple spiritual traditions:

  • Hinduism: The Bhagavad Gita's message of detached action and devotion inspires my daily discipline and purpose.
  • Buddhism: The teachings of impermanence, mindfulness, and compassion guide my ecological humility and non-attachment.
  • Christianity: The message of love, forgiveness, and sacrificial service echoes in my call to justice and environmental stewardship.
  • Islam: The Quranic verses—especially Surah Al-Fatiha and Ayat‑ul‑Kursi—offer me a constant reminder of divine mercy, guardianship, and unity.

Faith as a Bridge

For me, sacred texts are not walls that divide us—they are bridges across traditions. The Vedas, the Dhammapada, the Gospels, and the Quran each offer a lens into truth. I see these as complementary streams flowing toward the same ocean of divine understanding.

Harmonizing Knowledge and Wisdom

My journey has been one of integration—of blending rigorous science with timeless wisdom, and of seeking a planetary ethic rooted in both insight and inspiration. I walk with gratitude across traditions, believing that ecology and faith together can help heal the Earth and the human spirit.
