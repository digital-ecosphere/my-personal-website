About Me

Born in the Himalayas

I was naturally drawn to the intricate balance of nature and the wisdom it holds. Trained as a forester and ecologist, I specialize in remote sensing, utilizing advanced geospatial technologies to understand landscapes, environmental changes, and ecosystem dynamics. My expertise extends into artificial intelligence, where I integrate AI-driven insights to enhance environmental monitoring, conservation, and sustainable resource management.

Beyond my scientific pursuits, my journey is deeply rooted in spirituality. Coming from a Hindu family tradition, I have embraced the wisdom of multiple faiths—Christianity, Buddhism, and Islam—finding harmony in their shared values of compassion, mindfulness, service, and devotion to the Divine. To me, science and spirituality are not opposing forces but complementary perspectives that guide my work and my worldview. Whether studying the forests from above through remote sensing or seeking deeper truths through faith, I strive to bridge knowledge and wisdom for a more sustainable and meaningful future.

Science Meets Spirituality

Rooted in Hinduism as my family tradition, I have grown to embrace the wisdom and spiritual depth of Christianity, Buddhism, and Islam, seeing them not as contradictions but as different paths toward understanding the Divine. From Hinduism, I inherit a deep sense of devotion (Bhakti), the philosophy of unity (Advaita), and the sacred connection between the self and the universe. In Christianity, I find the call to love, grace, and selfless service, which align with the principles of dharma. In Buddhism, the practice of mindfulness and inner peace guides me toward self-discipline and compassion. In Islam, I embrace the profound surrender to the One God (Tawhid), the importance of justice, and the call to live a righteous life.

Rather than viewing these traditions as separate, I see them as different expressions of the same eternal truth—leading toward a life of faith, wisdom, and harmony. Across these traditions, I find inspiration in sacred words and prayers that uplift the soul. These powerful mantras, verses, and supplications remind me of the divine presence in every moment of life.

My Spiritual Inspirations

🕉️ Hinduism (Sanatana Dharma)

Gayatri Mantra (Rig Veda 3.62.10) – A universal prayer for wisdom and enlightenment:

Om Bhur Bhuvah Swaha,
Tat Savitur Varenyam,
Bhargo Devasya Dhīmahi,
Dhiyo Yo Nah Prachodayāt.

“We meditate upon the divine light of the Supreme Being; may it inspire and guide our intellect.”

Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra (For protection and spiritual awakening):

Om Tryambakam Yajamahe,
Sugandhim Pushtivardhanam,
Urvarukamiva Bandhanan,
Mrityor Mukshiya Maamritat.

“We worship the three-eyed Lord Shiva who is fragrant and nourishes all beings. May He liberate us from the bondage of death and grant us immortality.”

✝️ Christianity

The Lord’s Prayer (Matthew 6:9-13) – A prayer Jesus taught for guidance and devotion:

Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be thy name.
Thy kingdom come, thy will be done, on earth as it is in heaven.
Give us this day our daily bread,
and forgive us our trespasses, as we forgive those who trespass against us.
And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. Amen.

Psalm 23:1-4 (A prayer of faith and trust in God):

The Lord is my shepherd, I shall not want.
He makes me lie down in green pastures;
He leads me beside still waters;
He restores my soul.

☸️ Buddhism

The Buddha’s Metta (Loving-Kindness) Prayer – A prayer for compassion for all beings:

Sabbe Satta Sukhi Hontu

“May all beings be happy.”

The Heart Sutra Mantra (For wisdom and enlightenment):

Gate Gate Pāragate Pārasamgate Bodhi Svāhā!

“Gone, gone, gone beyond, gone altogether beyond—enlightenment, so be it!”

The Triple Gem Refuge (A fundamental Buddhist affirmation of faith):

Buddham Saranam Gacchami (I take refuge in the Buddha),
Dhammam Saranam Gacchami (I take refuge in the Dharma),
Sangham Saranam Gacchami (I take refuge in the Sangha).

☪️ Islam

Surah Al-Fatiha (1:1-7) – The Opening Chapter of the Quran (Most Recited Prayer in Islam)

Bismillahir Rahmanir Raheem,
Alhamdulillahi Rabbil 'Alamin,
Ar-Rahmanir-Raheem,
Maliki Yawmid-Deen,
Iyyaka Na’budu wa iyyaka nasta’een,
Ihdinas-Siratal-Mustaqeem,
Siratal-ladhina an’amta ‘alayhim, ghayril-maghdoobi ‘alayhim wa la-dhallin.

“In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. Praise be to Allah, Lord of all worlds. The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. Master of the Day of Judgment. You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help. Guide us to the straight path—the path of those whom You have blessed, not of those who have gone astray.”

Shahada (Declaration of Faith in Islam):

La ilaha illa Allah, Muhammadur Rasulullah.

“There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah.”

Ayat-ul-Kursi (Quran 2:255) – A Verse of Protection and Divine Power

Allahu La Ilaha illa Huwa, Al-Hayyul-Qayyum...

“Allah! There is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of existence...”

For me, these sacred words are not just prayers but a bridge between traditions—reminders of divine love, wisdom, and peace. Whether in the Vedic hymns, the Psalms, the Buddha’s teachings, or the Quranic verses, I find a universal call to truth, righteousness, and devotion. My spiritual journey is not about choosing one over another, but about deepening my connection with the Divine in all its expressions.
