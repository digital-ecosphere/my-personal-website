About Me

Born in the Himalayas

I was naturally drawn to the intricate balance of nature and the wisdom it holds. Trained as a forester and ecologist, I specialize in remote sensing and advanced geospatial technologies to understand landscapes, environmental changes, and ecosystem dynamics. My expertise extends into artificial intelligence, where I integrate AI-driven insights to enhance environmental monitoring, conservation, and sustainable resource management.

Beyond my scientific pursuits, my journey is deeply rooted in spirituality. Coming from a Hindu family tradition, I have embraced the wisdom of multiple faiths—Christianity, Buddhism, and Islam—finding harmony in their shared values of compassion, mindfulness, service, and devotion to the Divine. For me, science and spirituality complement each other, guiding both my work and worldview.

Science Meets Spirituality

Rooted in Hinduism as my family tradition, I have grown to embrace the wisdom and spiritual depth of Christianity, Buddhism, and Islam, seeing them as different paths toward understanding the Divine. From Hinduism, I inherit a sense of devotion (Bhakti) and unity (Advaita). In Christianity, I find the call to love and selfless service. In Buddhism, the practice of mindfulness and compassion guides me. In Islam, I value surrender to the One God (Tawhid) and the pursuit of justice and righteousness.

My Spiritual Inspirations

🕉️ Hinduism (Sanatana Dharma)

Gayatri Mantra (Rig Veda 3.62.10) – A universal prayer for wisdom and enlightenment:

Om Bhur Bhuvah Swaha,
Tat Savitur Varenyam,
Bhargo Devasya Dhīmahi,
Dhiyo Yo Nah Prachodayāt.

“We meditate upon the divine light of the Supreme Being; may it inspire and guide our intellect.”

Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra – A plea for protection and spiritual awakening:

Om Tryambakam Yajamahe,
Sugandhim Pushtivardhanam,
Urvarukamiva Bandhanan,
Mrityor Mukshiya Maamritat.

“May the three‑eyed Lord liberate us from bondage and grant immortality.”

✝️ Christianity

The Lord’s Prayer (Matthew 6:9‑13) – Taught by Jesus for guidance and devotion:

Our Father in heaven, hallowed be Your name…

Psalm 23 – A song of faith and trust:

The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want…

☸️ Buddhism

Metta Prayer – A wish for universal well‑being:

Sabbe Satta Sukhi Hontu — “May all beings be happy.”

Heart Sutra Mantra – A call to wisdom:

Gate Gate Pāragate Pārasamgate Bodhi Svāhā!

☪️ Islam

  • Surah Al‑Fatiha – Opening chapter invoking mercy and guidance.
  • Shahada – Declaration of faith: La ilaha illa Allah, Muhammadur Rasulullah.
  • Ayat‑ul‑Kursi – Verse of divine guardianship (Quran 2:255).

These prayers and verses serve as bridges between traditions—reminders of divine love, wisdom, and peace. Whether in the Vedic hymns, the Psalms, the Buddha’s teachings, or the Quranic verses, I hear a universal call to truth and compassion.

About Me

Born in the Himalayas

I grew up amid the Himalayan forests, trained as a forester and remote‑sensing ecologist, and now apply AI‑driven geospatial tools to monitor and protect our planet.

My approach blends rigorous science with spiritual reflection, guided by respect for creation and a commitment to sustainable stewardship.

Science & Spirituality

I explore how data‑driven insight and faith can work together to promote environmental justice and human well‑being.

Spiritual Inspiration

  • Surah Al‑Fatiha – Opening chapter; a daily call for mercy and guidance.
  • Shahada – Core declaration of faith.
  • Ayat‑ul‑Kursi – Verse of divine guardianship and protection.

Interfaith Reflection

For me, these sacred words are not just prayers but a bridge between traditions—reminders of divine love, wisdom, and peace. Whether in the Vedic hymns, the Psalms, the Buddha’s teachings, or the Quranic verses, I find a universal call to truth, righteousness, and devotion. My spiritual journey is not about choosing one over another, but about deepening my connection with the Divine in all its expressions.
